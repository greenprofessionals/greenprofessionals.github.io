Call for Member Volunteers

UNGA 81 High-Level Panel Concepts — September 21, 2026

The Executive Committee has approved Green Professionals' participation in the UNGA 81 High-Level Dialogue on Inclusive Leadership and the Future of the United Nations, convened alongside the Aspire Artemis Foundation event "Principled Leadership: Women at the Forefront." Consistent with our mandate under Article III of the Constitution to produce policy briefs and strengthen partnerships with international stakeholders, GP will develop original concepts for members to present at the panels and side dialogues.

This is an invitation for members to volunteer to develop and present a concept at the dialogue. Five illustrative concepts are outlined below to spark ideas — members are strongly encouraged to propose a different concept if they have one better suited to their expertise or interests. GP's Policy & Research Committee will provide full development support either way; volunteers are not expected to build a concept note alone.

Background reading: The full draft concept note for the UNGA 81 event, prepared by the Aspire Artemis Foundation, is available here: DRAFT-UNGA81 High Level Event Concept Note — Aspire Artemis Foundation, September 21st.docx

Illustrative Concepts — A Starting Point

These five are meant to introduce the range of angles available, not to limit your options. If you have a concept of your own in mind, propose it — see "How to Volunteer" below.

01

Diaspora Professionals as Financing Partners for Multilateral Priorities

Positioning diaspora remittances, technical expertise, and impact investment as an underused lever in closing the UN's humanitarian and development financing gap.

02

Women's Leadership in Governance: The Sierra Leone Model

A case study on Sierra Leone's recent gains in women's representation in senior government, offered as a concrete example within the "women at the forefront" theme.

03

Intergenerational Leadership: Youth as Co-Architects, Not Just Participants

Drawing on GP's own constitutional youth structures — the Youth Chapter, ex-officio Executive seat, and annual mentorship report — as a working model for meaningful youth inclusion.

04

Evidence-Based Policy from the Global South: Lessons for Multilateral Reform

Presenting GP's policy fellowship and research approach as a governance-innovation case for strengthening institutional legitimacy.

05

Diaspora Technical Exchange as a Model for South–North Knowledge Flow

A focused look at the mechanisms of skills and knowledge transfer between diaspora professionals and home-country institutions.

How to Volunteer

Members wishing to represent Green Professionals should submit the following by the deadline below:

  • Your topic — one of the illustrative concepts above, or a concept of your own proposal
  • A brief statement (2–3 sentences) describing the concept (if proposing your own) or your relevant expertise (if choosing one above)
  • Confirmation of availability for concept-development sessions ahead of September 21
Proposing your own concept? Great — that's welcomed. Submit a short working title and 2–3 sentence description along with your interest statement. The Executive Committee and Policy & Research Committee will review all proposals for fit and feasibility before confirming assignments.
Note: Eligibility is limited to Full Members in good standing, consistent with Article IX(5) of the Constitution. Where more than one member volunteers for the same concept, GP may pair volunteers as co-presenters rather than select a single representative.
